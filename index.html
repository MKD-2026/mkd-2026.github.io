First : Can You Donate?

It's important to determine whether your health will allow for a kidney donation. I'm working with Ochsner, who has provided the following questionnaire to check for common roadblocks to donating. Upon completing the questionnaire, a doctor will review the information and reach out if they believe you may be a viable donor.

Click the button below to access the questionnaire. It will request my information (shown to the left) after a few questions.

  • Ochsner Questionnaire

Second : Compatibility

After the questionnaire, the transplant team will work with you directly and inform you of any necessary steps, but I'll outline them below for those who wish to know ahead of time.

If a doctor determines from the questionnaire that you may be a candidate to donate, they will follow up and request tests to confirm compatibility and assess any medical concerns. Even if the tests determine that a direct kidney donation would be incompatible, as long as there are no health concerns Ochsner can work with the National Kidney Registry to swap the donor kidney with another kind donor who is a match anywhere in the nation.

  • Swap Program Information

Third : Information

If the previous tests confirm that being a donor is safe, the transplant team will aim to meet with you in person and inform you of any risks, procedures, and things to know regarding the transplant operation. Come prepared, as this will be a great opportunity to ask questions and clear concerns!

Final : Transplant Operation

The transplant operation is minimally invasive and has a fairly short recovery time. Usually 24 hours before walking around and a little over a month before returning to full physical activity.

The decision to follow through with the transplant operation can be uncomfortable and scary. If that's that's the choice you make, I struggle to adequately express how much it means to me. All I can say is thank you.
